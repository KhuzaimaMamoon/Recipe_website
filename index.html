White sauce pasta

Ingredients

  • Pasta
  • Milk
  • Cheese
  • Mix herbs
  • Butter
  • All-purpose-flour

Procedure

Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add an equal amount of all-purpose flour to create a roux, stirring constantly for 1-2 minutes. Gradually whisk in milk, ensuring a smooth consistency. Continue cooking and stirring until the sauce thickens. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg, then toss with cooked pasta for a creamy white sauce pasta.

Punjabi-Chole-Masala

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chole), soaked overnight
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp dried mango powder (amchur) ,Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil

Procedure

Boil soaked chickpeas in a pressure cooker until soft. Set aside. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté for 1-2 minutes. Stir in the pureed tomatoes, cook until oil separates from the masala. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala for a few minutes. Pour in the boiled chickpeas with a bit of their cooking water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until flavors meld. Sprinkle garam masala, dried mango powder, and chopped cilantro. Cook for an additional 5 minutes. Adjust salt and spice levels as needed. Serve hot with naan or rice for delicious Punjabi Chole Masala.

Best-Kadai-Paneer

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed 1 cup bell peppers (capsicum), thinly sliced 1 cup onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped 1 tsp cumin seeds 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste) 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) Salt to taste Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Cooking oil

Procedure

Heat oil in a kadai or wok, add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes. Add sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil separates from the masala. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala for a few minutes until the spices are well-incorporated. Add sliced bell peppers and cook until they are slightly tender yet retain a crunch. Gently fold in the paneer cubes, ensuring they are coated with the masala. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Sprinkle garam masala and kasuri methi, then give it a final stir. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or roti for a delicious Kadai Paneer.

Valentines-Day-Oreo-Truffles

Ingredients

  • package (about 36) Oreo cookies
  • 1 package (8 oz) cream cheese, softened
  • 1-2 cups white or dark chocolate chips, melted
  • Valentine's Day-themed sprinkles or decorations (optional)

Procedure

Place the Oreo cookies in a food processor and pulse until you have fine crumbs. If you don't have a food processor, you can place the cookies in a sealed plastic bag and crush them using a rolling pin. In a mixing bowl, combine the Oreo crumbs with softened cream cheese and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined and a dough-like consistency is formed. Scoop out small portions of the mixture and roll them into 1-inch balls. Place the truffles on a parchment-lined tray. Place the tray with the truffles in the refrigerator for about 15-20 minutes to allow them to firm up. In a heatproof bowl, melt the white or dark chocolate chips in the microwave or using a double boiler. Place the coated truffles back on the parchment-lined tray and refrigerate until the chocolate coating is set. Once the chocolate is firm, your Valentine's Day Oreo Truffles are ready to be served. Place them in festive cupcake liners or a decorative box for a lovely presentation. Enjoy with your special someone!
